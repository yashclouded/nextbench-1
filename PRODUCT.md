# Nextbench

## Product Purpose
A verified student-to-student marketplace for school communities. Buy, sell, and trade textbooks, notes, supplies, electronics, and more — safely within your own campus ecosystem. Replaces chaotic WhatsApp/Facebook groups and untrustworthy general marketplaces.

## Users
School students aged 13-19 (Grades 8-12, plus early college). Digital natives who value authenticity, social proof, and safety — and whose parents care deeply about all three. Not tech bros, not startup hustlers, not generic "users."

## Register
brand

## Brand Tone
Premium & Refined, but warm and accessible — never cold or austere. Think Apple's craft ethic crossed with Notion's friendliness and Monzo's approachable confidence. The vibe is "a student marketplace designed by people who actually respect students" — not patronizing, not infantilizing, not corporate.

## Emotional Goal
Trust & Safety above everything. The primary feeling when landing on Nextbench should be relief — *"this is safe, this is for people like me, my parents would approve."* Secondary: a quiet excitement about the campus economy opening up.

## Anti-References
- Do NOT look like a generic SaaS startup (the hero-metric template, gradient text, glassmorphism, identical card grids)
- Do NOT look like Facebook Marketplace or Craigslist (sketchy, uncurated, dangerous)
- Do NOT look like a toy (childish colors, bouncy animations, cartoon mascots)
- Do NOT look like a bank (sterile, cold, intimidating)
- Do NOT use the "big number, small label, gradient accent" hero-metric template

## Visual References
Notion, Monzo, Airtable — friendly premium. Warm neutrals, confident typography, rounded but purposeful corners, illustrations over photos where possible, trust signals baked into the design language itself.

## Strategic Principles
1. Every visual decision must answer: "does this make the user feel safer or less safe?"
2. Never compromise on perceived quality — teenagers can smell cheap design
3. Parents are a silent second audience — the page should reassure them too
4. Less is more: a premium marketplace earns trust through restraint, not features
5. The graduation/legacy narrative is a unique moat — it should feel like the emotional peak of the page
